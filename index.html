Hello! This is me

Hello everybody! I'm Anna and I create this web site for tell why I would like to go to Ireland. In the next chapters I put some information about me, together they create my good (I hope) reasons to go to Ireland this summer. Let's start!

Hello! This is me

What Experience I did before "Beyond all Borders" 

Before the project "Beyond all Borders", I did some travels with my family: I went to Spain, France, Croatia and Greece. In these experiences I understood how much English is important for communicate with people from all over the world. In 2021 my school selected me and other students for an English course to prepare us for the Cambridge Starters Exam (level preA1) and I passed it. Last year me and two of my classmates read a poetry at the Christmas concert in front of all the school: we were scared but also exited!

My abilities...

My biggest passion is reading books, all my house is full of my books and comics. But in the afternoon, when I'm not read, I'm playing basketball! I play it from 7 years. I started in Pozzoleone with some of my classmates but, when I was 11 years old, I went to Grantorto and I did basketball with a new team: they were only boys and they were 15 years old! Now I have got friends in Pozzoleone and also in Grantorto. In this years I also tried other activities. For example I did for 2 years a drama course with Theama Teatro company at the Auditorium Fonato in Thiene and I played piano for 4 years.

Finally, I would like to go to Ireland because...

And now, after a brief introduction about me, the most important chapter of the site web, the question of the year: Why would you like to go to Ireland? I would like to go to Ireland for 3 reasons:
- With this project I can learn and consolidate my English, I understood how much English is important during my travels (chapter 2).
- I have a big opportunity: meet new people (chapter 3) with a different culture, I'm curiose to see how people live at thousands Kilometres to me.
-Another, important reason is the complete autonomy. There are adults with me, but I'm in a new nation without my parents and I have to be responsible and talk English well alone, without someone who talk for me to other people (not like chapter 2!).
